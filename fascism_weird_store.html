Why is fascism weird?

Fascism strips rights, targets communities, and spreads cruelty. It's also ridiculous, fragile, and built on obvious lies. Here's why calling it weird undermines its power.

It asks you to believe fictions instead of reality

Fascism depends on people choosing stories over their own experience. Propaganda repeats until the unreal feels safer to believe than the truth. It creates a "hyperreal" world where symbols and slogans matter more than facts.

It pretends to be strong, but it's fragile

Despite claiming moral clarity and strength, fascism collapses under questions, jokes, or even small cracks in its image of control. Its "intrinsic morality" can't withstand genuine inquiry or satire.

It invents the fire so it can pretend to put it out

Strongmen thrive by manufacturing crises. The chaos makes them look necessary, but it's a dumb loop of destruction and false solutions. They create problems specifically to pose as the only answer.

It turns politics into a cult ritual

Leaders stoke fear, followers chant slogans, and real social problems are ignored in favor of ritual blame and hollow promises. Complex issues like healthcare, poverty, and inequality get replaced with scapegoating ceremonies.

It calls cruelty virtue

Instead of reducing suffering, fascism glorifies cruelty as strength. Violence, exclusion, and harm are framed as moral duty. A healthy society should measure decisions by whether they reduce sufferingâ€”fascism does the opposite.

It sells itself like a brand

Hats, shirts, slogans, even branded religious textsâ€”fascism markets itself like a lifestyle brand. Propaganda becomes merch, and loyalty becomes something you wear. It borrows directly from marketing techniques to turn ideology into identity.

Why dismissal works

Fascism needs spectacle and fear to function. It thrives on being taken seriously, even by its opponents. Calling it weird strips away that aura of inevitability and importance. Dismissal denies fascism the legitimacy it craves while still acknowledging the real harm it causes.
